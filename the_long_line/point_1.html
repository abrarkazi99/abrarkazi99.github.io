Sets and Numbers

Let \(a,b\in\mathbb{N}\). Then there exist disjoint sets \(A,B\) such that \(a=|A|,b=|B|\). Then \(a+b:=|A\cup B|\). Now we can prove the elementary properties of addition.

Axioms

  1. Commutativity: \(a+b=b+a\)
  2. Associativity: \((a+b)+c=a+(b+c)\)

Commutativity will follow from the fact that the union operation on sets is commutative, \(A\cup B = B\cup A\). \[A\cup B = \{x|x\in A \lor x\in B\}=\{x|x\in B\lor x\in A\}=B\cup A.\] So union is commutative, and this followed from the commutativity of disjuction. This means the commutativity of addition ultimately boils down to commutativity of disjuction. How cool is that? Now let's try proving associativity. This one's gonna be a bit more involved.
